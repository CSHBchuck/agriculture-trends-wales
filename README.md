# Analyzing Long-Term Agricultural Trends in Wales (1990–2025)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes long-term trends in **livestock production** and **land use** in Wales using official government data from 1990 to 2025. The goal is to identify key patterns and provide actionable insights for farmers, agribusinesses, and agricultural technology providers.

## Business Problem

Welsh agriculture has undergone major structural changes over the past 35 years. This analysis aims to:
- Understand shifts in livestock and land use
- Identify growth sectors and areas of decline
- Highlight opportunities for data-driven decision making and agtech solutions

## Key Findings

### Livestock Trends

**1990 → 2025:**
- **Poultry**: +72.2% (strong and consistent growth)
- **Sheep & lambs**: -19.4%
- **Cattle & calves**: -20.8%
- **Pigs**: -72.2% (sharpest decline)

**2015 → 2025 (Recent Trends):**
- **Poultry**: +54.1% (continued strong growth)
- **Pigs**: +10.9% (recovery)
- **Cattle & calves**: -3.5%
- **Sheep & lambs**: -7.3%

### Land Use Trends

**1990 → 2025:**
- **Total arable crops**: +48.0%
- **Permanent pasture**: +19.7%
- **Total area farmed**: +9.2%
- **Rough grazing**: -13.8%

**2015 → 2025:**
- **Total arable crops**: +26.0% (strong recent growth)
- **Total area farmed**: +2.5%
- **Permanent pasture**: +1.2%

## Insights & Recommendations

### Key Insights
- Poultry farming has been the main growth driver in Welsh livestock over the past 35 years.
- Traditional livestock sectors (sheep, cattle, and pigs) have declined significantly.
- Arable farming has expanded notably, especially since 2015.
- Land use is gradually shifting toward more intensive and diversified production.

### Recommendations

**For Agtech Companies:**
- Strong opportunity in **poultry production efficiency** tools and **arable crop optimization**.
- Demand likely exists for data-driven solutions that support farmers transitioning between traditional and modern systems.

**For Farmers:**
- Consider diversification toward high-growth areas (poultry and arable crops).
- Evaluate precision agriculture tools to improve efficiency.

**For Policymakers:**
- Support technology adoption and skills development in growing sectors.
- Provide targeted assistance for regions affected by declining livestock numbers.
